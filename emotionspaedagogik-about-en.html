How it began

Development of the method by Katrin Michl

Professional foundation

Teaching in schools and working with learners revealed how emotions directly affect learning, safety, cooperation, and leadership in everyday professional life.

Professional contexts

Professional contexts involved crisis, conflict, responsibility, and boundary-setting highlighted the need for emotional competence that goes beyond intuition.

Method development

Research, teaching practice, and applied experience were translated into clear, repeatable tools for noticing, regulating, and using emotions constructively in professional roles.

Institute foundation

The Private Institute for Emotional Pedagogy was founded to offer recognised training programmes that strengthen emotional competence where it is most needed: in people who guide others.

Who we help

Who we help

If you carry emotional responsibility for others, this training strengthens the way you show up.

Leaders in education settings & teachers

De-escalate tension in classrooms and meetings, set clear boundaries with students and parents, and stay steady in conflict situations.

Social & health professionals

Hold emotionally loaded situations with clients, patients and families, without losing your own stability or overstepping your role.

HR & people managers

Lead difficult conversations, performance, conflict, restructuring and team care with clarity and support healthier team dynamics.

Team & project leads in NGOs, public sector and organisations

Guide teams through pressure, change and crisis, while keeping orientation and emotional safety in the group.

Self-employed professionals in helping roles

Therapists, counsellors, trainers and consultants who want a clear, recognised framework for the emotional side of their work — beyond personal intuition.
