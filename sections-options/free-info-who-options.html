Free Info Session — Who It's For Options

Current issues: flat cream-on-cream, no card containment, check/X circles are small and low-weight, large gap between heading and content, columns feel disconnected. All options add proper containment and visual hierarchy.

Option A
Split layout — heading left, stacked cards right
Consistent with FAQ, curriculum, and training path split patterns. Heading + intro sticky left. Right side: two stacked cards — "Perfect if" with pink left border, "Not for you" with muted border. Line dividers between items. Most consistent with the page's design language.

Who it's for (and not for)

This session is designed for people in professional responsibility roles. Here's how to tell if it fits your situation.

Perfect if…
✓

You carry emotional responsibility for others in your work — e.g. school leadership, teaching, social and health professions, HR, team leads, or self-employed experts.

✓

You want recognised, practical professional development — with clarity on how the IHK path fits your role.

✓

You prefer structured, non-therapeutic tools that you can explain to colleagues, supervisors, or HR.

Not for you if…
✗

You're looking for therapy, clinical treatment, or crisis support.

✗

You dislike live sessions and only want self-paced videos.

✗

Recognised qualifications and certificates are not important for you or your organisation.

Option B
Two cards side by side — contained columns
Same two-column layout but each column sits inside a white card with colored top border. "Perfect if" gets pink top, "Not for you" gets muted top. Heading above both cards. More contained than current — the cards create clear visual boundaries. Keeps the equal-weight comparison.
